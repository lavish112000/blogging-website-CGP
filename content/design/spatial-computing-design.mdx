## 1) Design evolution

We moved from command lines (1D) to GUIs (2D) to mobile (touch). Spatial computing (3D/XR) is the next layer: interfaces that exist in the real world.

## 2) Core principle

The core principle is **environmental respect**.

- **Scale:** UI should feel appropriate to the physical distance.
- **Occlusion:** virtual objects should respect real objects.
- **Input:** gaze, gesture, and voice replace the mouse.

## 5) Key Insights & Trends (2025)

With the proliferation of mixed reality headsets, **Spatial Computing** has become a distinct design discipline. In 2025, designers are mastering the art of creating interfaces that exist in 3D space, dealing with depth, occlusion, and hand-eye interaction models.

**Key Trends:**
*   **Volumetric Interfaces:** UI elements are gaining depth and volume, moving away from flat planes floating in space to objects that feel tangible and interactive.
*   **Gaze and Pinch Interaction:** The standard interaction model has settled on eye-tracking for selection and hand gestures for action, requiring large, forgiving hit targets and clear visual feedback.

**Data Points:**
*   Demand for specialized **spatial computing designers** grew by **200%** in 2025 following the release of next-gen headsets from major tech giants.

## 6) Accessibility considerations

Spatial computing has high barriers.

- **Motor control:** gestures can be hard for some users. Support alternative inputs (eye, voice).
- **Visual impairment:** depth perception varies. Don€™t rely solely on depth cues.
- **Safety:** ensure users don€™t trip or hit real objects.

## 6) Common mistakes
